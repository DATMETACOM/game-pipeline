# IDEA ANALYSIS: DogFlow

## Analysis Under Voodoo Framework (VR01-VR06)

---

## VR01. Clear Puzzle Core

### Initial Raw Idea

The raw pitch had three attractive pieces:
- a visible flock running toward danger
- sheepdogs affecting the flock's movement
- a rescue goal that ends in safe pens

### Main Risk

The first drafted version leaned too hard on line drawing and could read as:

`Save the Dog, but with sheep`

That was too close to an existing literacy shell and not strong enough as a standalone puzzle identity.

### Revision Decision

The concept is now framed as:

`Place sheepdogs so they turn the flock into the correct pens.`

This keeps the game easier to read for hybrid casual players and makes the dog fantasy central instead of decorative.

---

## VR02. Newness

### What Is Familiar

- Cute-animal rescue setup
- Moving units with visible danger
- Placement puzzle logic that resolves into a cinematic outcome

### What Is New

The actual twist is:

`pre-place sheepdogs to redirect the flock before it runs`

The player is not actively drawing a defensive wall. The player is setting up living blockers that shape the flock's path.

### Voodoo Fit

This follows the safer Voodoo formula:
- familiar visual logic
- one clear new behavior
- readable stakes in motion

---

## VR03. Fair Challenge and APS

### Main Risk

If the sheep or dogs behave like loose simulation agents, the game stops feeling fair.

That would break one of Voodoo's key requirements:
- readable strategy
- fair failure
- no hidden information

### Revision Decision

The prototype should use:
- setup phase first
- release phase second
- deterministic sheep speed and turning rules
- visible direction arrows before release
- visible dog influence line or influence zone, even if the rope fades visually

This keeps the player's decisions legible and supports APS tuning.

### APS Outlook

The concept can hit a healthy range if difficulty comes from:
- dog placement count
- flock split decisions
- pen capacity
- one-way terrain
- timing of collapsing blockers

The concept should avoid:
- random herd behavior
- hidden spawn changes
- physics-heavy collisions as the primary challenge

---

## VR04. Game Feel

### Strong Potential

The concept has stronger feel now because the core visual is more character-driven:
- dog sprints into position
- flock bends and bunches as direction changes
- safe pens fill in a clean wave
- the final dog closure feels like real herding

### Pixar-Polish Direction

The dog and sheep can carry the premium emotional layer:
- expressive faces
- clean ranch world
- soft materials
- readable danger
- satisfying success burst when the last sheep enters the final slot

This keeps the Pixar-polish idea alive without needing an abstract board-first wrapper.

---

## VR05. Fast Prototype Scope

### Good News

The idea still fits a compact V1:
- one flock type
- one dog type
- two or three pens
- one release button
- four obstacles maximum

### Scope Guardrails

Do not add:
- dog upgrades
- farm building
- wolf enemies
- weather systems
- revive economy in prototype

The dog should remain a simple gameplay tool with one consistent behavior.

---

## VR06. Publishability

### Why It Reads Fast

The ad frame is simple:
- sheep are moving
- danger is obvious
- pens are visible
- dogs are placed and the flock changes direction

### Why It Can Win Attention

The concept has two easy angles:
- fail ad: one sheep slips past the dog line and falls
- smart ad: the dog placement cleanly bends the whole flock into safety

This is broad enough for Voodoo-style testing without leaning on a saturated market wrapper.

---

## Summary of Revision Choices

| Raw Direction | Revised Direction |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Draw-to-save clone risk | Dog-placement herding puzzle |
| Dog as visual layer | Dog as core interaction |
| Real-time chaos risk | Plan first, release second |
| General rescue game | Cinematic flock redirection |

---

## Final Recommendation

### Decision

Approved for Prototype V1.

### Why

- Clear core in one sentence
- Different enough from pure draw-defense games
- Strong 3-second readability
- Voodoo-fit scope
- APS can be tuned with readable difficulty knobs

### What Must Be Protected During Production

- The game must stay puzzle-first.
- Dog behavior must stay simple and deterministic.
- Flock turning rules must stay readable.
- Presentation must support understanding, not hide the system.
